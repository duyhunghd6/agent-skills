# SAFe Roles & Responsibilities

SAFe defines clear roles at team, ART, and portfolio levels. Each role has distinct accountability for value delivery.

## Team-Level Roles

### Scrum Master (SM)

Servant leader for a single Agile team. Coaches the team on Agile practices and removes impediments.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Facilitate team-level ceremonies (standups, retrospectives, iteration planning)
- Shield the team from interruptions and scope creep during iterations
- Coach on Scrum/Kanban practices and Built-in Quality
- Escalate impediments to the RTE when cross-team resolution is needed

✅ **Do**: Focus on team health and process improvement
❌ **Don't**: Assign tasks or act as a project manager

### Product Owner (PO)

The empowered voice of the customer for a single team. Owns the team backlog.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Define, prioritize, and maintain the team backlog
- Write user stories with clear acceptance criteria
- Accept or reject completed work based on Definition of Done
- Collaborate with Product Management on feature decomposition
- Participate in PI Planning to break features into team-level stories

✅ **Do**: Make daily prioritization decisions for the team
✅ **Do**: Stay connected to customers and stakeholders
❌ **Don't**: Dictate implementation details to developers

### Developers & Testers

Cross-functional team members who design, build, test, and deploy.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Self-organize to deliver committed PI Objectives
- Practice TDD, pair programming, code review, and CI
- Participate actively in PI Planning breakout sessions
- Own technical quality through collective code ownership

## ART-Level Roles

### Release Train Engineer (RTE)

Chief Scrum Master and servant leader for the entire ART.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Facilitate PI Planning, ART Sync, System Demo, and Inspect & Adapt
- Coach Scrum Masters and ART leadership
- Track and communicate PI progress against objectives
- Manage program-level risks and impediments
- Escalate to Solution Train or Portfolio when needed

✅ **Do**: Own the ART's process effectiveness across all teams
❌ **Don't**: Become a command-and-control program manager

### Product Manager (PM)

Owns the ART backlog and the product vision/roadmap.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Define the product vision and present it at PI Planning
- Maintain the ART (Program) backlog with prioritized Features
- Work with Business Owners to align on strategic themes
- Decompose Epics into Features; collaborate with POs on story splitting
- Define and validate PI Objectives with stakeholders

### System Architect / Engineer

Guards the system's architectural integrity across all teams.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Define architectural runway and enabler features
- Ensure non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability) are addressed
- Participate in PI Planning to guide technical feasibility
- Maintain and communicate the Solution Intent

### Business Owners

Senior stakeholders with ultimate accountability for business outcomes.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- Set strategic direction and priorities at PI Planning
- Assign business value to PI Objectives
- Participate in PI Planning and Inspect & Adapt
- Approve or redirect the ART's trajectory each PI

## Portfolio-Level Roles

### Epic Owner

Champions a portfolio-level Epic through the Portfolio Kanban.

### Lean Portfolio Management (LPM) Team

Governs strategy, funding, and guardrails across value streams.

## Anti-Patterns

❌ Having POs and PMs be the same person (conflates tactical and strategic ownership)
❌ RTE acting as a traditional project manager giving assignments
❌ Business Owners attending only PI Planning kick-off but not the full event
❌ No System Architect, leading to erosion of architectural integrity
